Why most brightening serums don't work

Dark spots and uneven tone form when your skin overproduces melanin — often triggered by sun exposure, hormonal shifts, or inflammation beneath the surface.

Most vitamin C serums use water-soluble ascorbic acid. It sits on top of the skin, oxidises quickly, and never reaches the deeper layers where pigmentation actually forms.

Sunlight Serum takes a different approach. Two hero ingredients. Two mechanisms. Working together below the surface.

Hero Ingredient 1
Organic Kashmiri Saffron

The problem: Excess melanin clusters beneath the surface, forming visible dark spots and uneven patches.

How it works: Saffron's active compounds help slow excess melanin production that contributes to visible pigmentation. Used for centuries across Indian skin science for luminous, even-toned skin.

The result: Existing dark spots gradually fade. New marks are less likely to form. Your natural tone comes through.

Hero Ingredient 2
Oil-Soluble Vitamin C

The problem: Standard water-soluble Vitamin C oxidises on contact with air and can irritate sensitive skin.

How it works: Ascorbyl Tetraisopalmitate stays stable, absorbs fully, and reaches the deeper skin layers where pigmentation begins. It supports collagen production and interrupts oxidative stress that triggers melanin overproduction — without redness or stinging.

The result: Brighter, more radiant skin that builds over weeks of consistent use. Gentle enough for sensitive and melanin-rich skin.

Sunlight Serum uses oil-soluble Vitamin C, which is significantly gentler than the water-soluble ascorbic acid found in most serums. No stinging, no redness. Formulated for all skin types, including sensitive and reactive skin.

Yes. Saffron has been used across Indian skin science for centuries specifically for deeper and melanin-rich skin tones. This serum does not bleach or lighten — it helps even out excess pigmentation while supporting your natural tone.
